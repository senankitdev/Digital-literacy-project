Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

1. Never share your OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank or official organization.

2. Do not approve unknown “collect requests” on UPI apps. Remember: receiving money does NOT require entering your PIN.

3. Avoid scanning random QR codes sent by strangers. Scanning a QR code can lead to money being debited from your account.

4. Always verify the identity of callers or messages claiming to offer rewards, refunds, or urgent help. Do not trust unknown numbers blindly.

5. Use strong and unique passwords for your email, social media, and banking apps. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.

6. Do not click on suspicious links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email, as they may lead to phishing websites.

7. Keep your mobile apps and operating system updated to protect against security vulnerabilities.

8. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions. If necessary, use a secure VPN connection.

9. Regularly check your bank statements and transaction history to quickly detect any unauthorized activity.

10. If you become a victim of cyber fraud, immediately report it to the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in or call the helpline number 1930.

Stay alert, think before you act, and always prioritize your digital safety.
